Nationale Game Museum

The National Videogame Museum is 'the place to be' for anyone who wants to know (and experience) more about the history, social and cultural side of video games. The museum also lets its visitors experience the games of tomorrow. experience. Gamers of all ages are welcome to play, discover, learn and share experiences online.

A garage box in The Hague is where the adventure began in 2008, with two friends Hasan Tasdemir and Pascal Rappailles. The garage box soon became too small after which they moved to a shed on an industrial estate. Unfortunately, the shed was not heated and everything had to be moved to an empty Ministry of Agriculture. When this building too had to be vacated, the then collection of 10 arcade video games with some spare parts was stored in an old classic car showroom. Since there was no room there to renovate or play the cabinets, suitable space was sought out

That was found in 2011 at the Cobaltstraat in Zoetermeer. There Hasan decided to rent the location with 4 friends and the adventure could grow into their own meeting place to practice their hobby and relax with a game.
